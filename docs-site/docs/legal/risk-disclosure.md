# Risk Disclosure Statement

## Important Notice

Trading foreign exchange, contracts for difference (CFDs), cryptocurrencies, and other leveraged products carries a high level of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. You should carefully consider your investment objectives, level of experience, and risk appetite before making any investment decisions.

:::danger High Risk Warning
You could lose some or all of your initial investment. Do not invest money that you cannot afford to lose.
:::

## General Risks

### Loss of Capital

- You may lose some or all of your invested capital
- Losses can exceed your initial deposit when trading with leverage
- Past performance is not indicative of future results
- No trading strategy is guaranteed to be profitable

### Market Risk

Financial markets are volatile and unpredictable:
- Prices can move against your position rapidly
- Market gaps can occur (especially over weekends)
- Slippage may cause orders to execute at different prices
- Liquidity can dry up during market stress

### Leverage Risk

Leverage amplifies both gains and losses:

```
Example with 1:100 leverage:
- Account balance: $1,000
- Position size: $100,000
- 1% adverse move = $1,000 loss (100% of capital)
```

**Important**: Higher leverage means:
- Greater potential profits
- Greater potential losses
- Margin calls can occur quickly
- Positions may be liquidated automatically

## Product-Specific Risks

### Forex (Foreign Exchange)

- **Currency risk**: Exchange rates fluctuate constantly
- **Political risk**: Government policies affect currencies
- **Interest rate risk**: Central bank decisions impact rates
- **Liquidity risk**: Some pairs have low liquidity
- **24-hour market**: Gaps can occur on market open

### Cryptocurrencies

- **Extreme volatility**: Prices can swing 10-20% in minutes
- **Regulatory risk**: Government regulations are evolving
- **Technology risk**: Blockchain and network issues
- **Security risk**: Exchange hacks and wallet theft
- **Market manipulation**: Unregulated markets susceptible to manipulation
- **No investor protection**: Limited regulatory oversight

### Commodities

- **Weather risk**: Agricultural commodities affected by weather
- **Storage costs**: Physical commodities incur storage fees
- **Seasonal variations**: Prices vary by season
- **Geopolitical risk**: Supply disruptions from conflicts
- **Production risk**: Mining/drilling operations can fail

### Indices

- **Market correlation**: Indices follow overall market trends
- **Constituent changes**: Index composition changes periodically
- **Dividend adjustments**: Payouts affect index levels
- **Economic sensitivity**: Affected by economic data releases

### Stocks

- **Company-specific risk**: Individual company performance
- **Earnings risk**: Quarterly results cause volatility
- **Sector risk**: Industry-wide factors affect prices
- **Liquidity risk**: Some stocks trade infrequently
- **Delisting risk**: Companies can be removed from exchanges

## Technical Risks

### Platform Risk

- Internet connectivity issues may prevent trading
- Platform outages may occur (though rare)
- Order execution may be delayed
- Prices displayed may differ from actual execution prices
- Technical failures could result in losses

### Execution Risk

- **Slippage**: Orders may execute at different prices than expected
- **Requotes**: Requested price may no longer be available
- **Partial fills**: Large orders may be partially executed
- **Rejected orders**: Orders may be rejected due to various reasons
- **Network latency**: Delays in order transmission

## Operational Risks

### Margin Calls

When your margin level falls below required levels:
1. You'll receive a margin call notification
2. You must deposit additional funds
3. If margin not restored, positions may be closed automatically
4. Losses may exceed your account balance

### Auto-Liquidation

Positions may be automatically closed when:
- Margin level falls below 50%
- Account reaches stop-out level
- Risk management rules triggered
- Market conditions deteriorate rapidly

### Account Security

Risks related to your account:
- Unauthorized access if credentials compromised
- Phishing attacks targeting your account
- Social engineering attempts
- Malware on your devices

## Regulatory Considerations

### Jurisdiction-Specific Risks

- Different jurisdictions have different regulations
- Investor protection varies by country
- Tax obligations vary by residence
- Legal recourse depends on jurisdiction

### Regulatory Changes

- New regulations may affect trading conditions
- Leverage limits may be reduced
- Products may become unavailable
- Compliance requirements may increase

## Recommendations

### Risk Management

1. **Never invest money you cannot afford to lose**
2. **Use stop-loss orders on every trade**
3. **Limit position sizes to 1-2% of capital per trade**
4. **Understand leverage and use it cautiously**
5. **Diversify across instruments and strategies**
6. **Keep adequate capital reserves**
7. **Monitor positions regularly**
8. **Have a written trading plan**

### Education

Before trading:
- Understand the products you're trading
- Learn technical and fundamental analysis
- Practice on a demo account
- Read our [Trading Guide](/docs/trading-guide/overview)
- Attend educational webinars
- Consult with financial advisors if needed

### Suitability Assessment

Trading may not be suitable if you:
- Cannot afford to lose your investment
- Need guaranteed returns
- Don't understand the risks involved
- Have limited trading experience
- Cannot monitor positions regularly
